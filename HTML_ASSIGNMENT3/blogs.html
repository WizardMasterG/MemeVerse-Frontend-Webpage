Welcome to Memeverse

Instagram
  • Most trending meme platform
  • Memes are often designed using screenshots, relatable photos, or pop culture scenes
  • Great reach via hashtags
  • Relies heavily on images, short videos, and meme templates with overlaid text.
  • Helps memes spread rapidly across friend groups and communities.
  • Includes relatable humor, sarcasm, deep-fried memes, absurdist humor, and wholesome content.
  • Instagram Reels introduced video-based memes with trending sounds.
  • Influencers and companies use memes for marketing and engagement.

Facebook
  • Wide user base for older memes
  • Classic meme format: image with top-and-bottom text captions.
  • Still has large meme following
  • Meme groups (public/private) are a huge hub for content.
  • Memes range from wholesome family jokes to dark humor and Gen Z sarcasm.
  • Long comment threads sometimes become meme content themselves.
  • Unlike Twitter, Facebook allows memes to be part of longer posts or rants.
  • Rage comics, Minion quotes, and Impact font memes still circulate widely.
  • Nostalgia-driven meme culture.

Twitter
  • Fastest meme trends
  • Memes are often based on clever, short text within 280 characters.
  • Good for meme reactions
  • Hashtags like #relatable, #memes, and #TwitterHumor help spread memes.
  • Widely shared across platforms like Instagram and Reddit.
  • Twitter users remix, quote-tweet, or build on each other’s jokes.
  • Celebrities, brands, and even politicians use memes for PR and fan interaction.
  • Users discover trends easily and join in with their own versions.
  • Includes puns, jokes, sarcasm, or parody tweets.
